The Taj Mahal with its arches, domes and intricate carvings, is considered one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture. Its floors and walkways use contrasting tiles or blocks in tessellation patterns. Tessellation is the process of creating a plane surface using the repetition of a geometric shape with no overlaps and no gaps.
